WEEK 3: BLOG POST

User Alignment

The kind of users that I want to attract to my website are people interested in the work I do such as animation, which they will see in my portfolio; potential developers and designers looking for scripts, where they will see my script page; and readers that are interested in my blogs, reflections and the journey to creating this website.

The goal is to inspire viewers to explore what I do and eventually contact me for a service—ideally, the users are those looking for someone with my skillset. To align with users, my site will be easy to navigate, visually appealing (I’m going for something cute but elegant), and clearly structured with no confusion about where to find things.

Mapping Out Content, Experience, and Interface Elements

Content Mapping

  • Homepage: Introduction, explore section, About Me, and visual background featuring my own work.
  • Portfolio Page: Showcasing academic and personal animation and illustration work.
  • Blog Page: Blog posts, reflections, updates, and written thoughts.
  • Design Page: Wireframes, color palettes, fonts, and UI design elements.
  • Script Page: JavaScript and CSS experiments and documentation.

Experience Mapping

  • Navigation: Persistent dashboard on every page.
  • User Journey: Homepage → About Me → Scroll to Explore → Click Portfolio → View Work → Contact Me.
  • Accessibility & Usability: Clear call-to-action buttons, intuitive flow, mobile responsiveness.

Interface Elements

  • Navigation Bar linking to all core sections.
  • Buttons: “Read More,” “View Portfolio.”

Structure, URL Scheme, and Semantic Markup

URL Scheme

  • Portfolio Page: mywebsite.com/portfolio
  • Blog Page: mywebsite.com/blog
  • Individual Blogs: mywebsite.com/blog/blogposts
  • Design Page: mywebsite.com/design
  • Script Page: mywebsite.com/scripts

Semantic Markup

  • <header> – For the main navigation
  • <main> – Primary content container
  • <section> – Grouping related information
  • <article> – Each blog post

How It All Comes Together

  • User alignment ensures the site meets visitors’ goals and expectations.
  • Content, experience, and interface planning build an engaging and functional site.
  • Semantic markup improves structure, readability, and accessibility.

Development Progress of Last Week

I made solid progress last week. I’ve created the basic look of the website and produced a wireframe for what I want it to become. The ideas may evolve over time, but for now, I feel confident and proud of the progress I’ve made.
